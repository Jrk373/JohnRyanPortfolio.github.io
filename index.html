Homelessness In Northern Arizona

AN IN DEPTH ANALYSIS OF HOMELESSNESS, SUBSTANCE USE, AND RECOVERY HOUSING PROJECTS

This research compared the prevalence of homelessness and substance use disorders among Arizona Medicaid members and compared the results to US Census data. The study also assessed the implementation of Oxford House, Inc. housing programs across Northern Arizona.

This research found that the prevalence of homelessness and substance use disorders was disproportionate to US Census data, and that pressure from state regulators led to implmentation of housing projects in areas with less need.

NCQA Follow Up after Hospitalization
Validation Model

This project developed a Validation Model to assess the results of third-party black box measurement tools for HEDIS-NCQA Value Based Purchasing programs.

The research revealed that over the course of a measurement year, providers were underrated by as much as 8 percentage points.

February 2, 2023

ICD10 Diagnostic Groupings

A predictive analysis of the effect of diagnostic groupings on high cost claims.

This study analyzed one year of inpatient behavioral health claims in order to assess if particular groupings of ICD10 diagnoses were more likely to be assiciated with high cost inpatient claims.

The study found that Mood Disorder and Psychotic disorders were predictive of over 90% of high cost claims. Subsequent research, including the VBP Validation Reports for FUH, have further confirmed these results in real time.
